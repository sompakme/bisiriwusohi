How Often Does Jeremy Renner Actually See His Daughter Ava After His Split From Sonni Pacheco?

Publish date: 2024-09-14

Celebrity romances are often as unpredictable as the characters they play on the big screen. The short-lived marriage of Hollywood heartthrob Jeremy Renner and his ex-wife Sonni Pacheco is no exception. Jeremy Renner and Pacheco's daughter, Ava was born on the 28th of March 2013, almost a year before the duo got married in January 2014. The union was brief, with the couple calling it quits just ten months later.

Their separation was far from amicable as the couple engaged in a bitter custody battle that lasted for years following their divorce in 2015. The drama between the former couple played out in the public eye, with fans and critics alike wondering what went wrong in their whirlwind romance. Despite the turmoil, Renner has remained resolute in his love for his daughter Ava, whom he shares with Pacheco.

Why Did Jeremy Renner And Sonni Pacheco Get Divorced?

In December 2015, Jeremy Renner and Sonni Pacheco an actress and model reached a divorce agreement. Pacheco requested that their prenuptial agreement be nullified because she claimed it was founded on "fraud" and cited irreconcilable differences as the cause of their breakup. She also requested spousal support and full physical custody of their daughter Ava.

When Pacheco filed for divorce, there was a fight right away she requested Renner return her passport, which she claimed was stolen, as well as her birth certificate and social security card in court documents that were submitted.

But the Avengers star in response filed a separate court document in February 2015 asking for shared custody of their daughter whose custody was ultimately split between the two, with Renner providing $13,000 in child support each month.

Pacheco had also accused Renner of threatening to harm her and himself, and of subjecting her to verbal, emotional, and substance abuse. The accusations were made in a 2019 court document as part of their custody dispute.

Does Jeremy Renner Loves Spending Time With His Daughter?

Being a Hollywood actor can be a demanding and time-consuming job, but for Renner, being a dad to Ava is his top priority. Describing his feelings when Ava was born, Renner claimed It was like seeing The Matrix, in a second everything just opened up and made perfect sense.

Since his split from Pacheco, Renner has made it his mission to cherish the limited time he has with Ava. In November 2021, Renner shared his thoughts on fatherhood and the importance of being there for his daughter in an interview with Men's Health.

He spoke candidly about the happiness that being a father brings him and how it has transformed his life beyond his expectations. He also mentioned that his daughter is his most cherished blessing.

The Marvel actor revealed that he flew nearly every weekend for 18 months from London to Los Angeles to spend time with Ava. However, he eventually decided to tell studios to work with his schedule, no matter the consequences.

"It taught me how to have the nuts to say, 'Everyone, f--k off. It's my time with my daughter," Renner said, adding that he'd been warned that the studios could fire him. "I said, Fine, recast me. I'm going to be here with my daughter. It was pretty gnarly."

What Jeremy Renner's Instagram Revealed About His Relationship With His Daughter Ava Berlin Renner

The Mayor of Kingston star often shares glimpses of Ava's life via Instagram, showcasing his love for his daughter and his doting dad skills. His Instagram also reveals he enjoys more low-key activities with his little girl, such as letting her give him a makeover.

Has Jeremy Renner Seen His Daughter Ava Berlin Renner Since His Near-Fatal Accident?

Jeremy Renner had been involved in a devastating accident involving a six-tonne snowplow near his home by the Nevada-California state border in the US in an attempt to save his nephew in January 2023. This wouldn't be the first time the actor has been in such a disastrous predicament as he had suffered significant injuries on a movie set before. But this particular incident was no movie.
