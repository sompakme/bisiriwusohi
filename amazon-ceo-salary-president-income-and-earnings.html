Amazon CEO Salary President Income and Earnings

Publish date: 2024-08-15

In 2022, Andrew R. Jassy, the President and Chief Executive Officer at AMAZON.COM INC, received a total compensation of $1,298,723. This included a salary of $317,500 and other forms of compensation totaling $981,223. Although Jassy’s salary was significantly higher than the median employee pay of $34,195, his compensation was much lower than his predecessor, Jeff Bezos.

Let’s dive deeper into the breakdown of Jassy’s compensation and explore the importance of understanding executive pay practices.

Comparison with Previous CEO

In 2022, Andrew R. Jassy’s compensation of $1.3 million was significantly lower than his predecessor, Jeff Bezos, who earned $212 million in the prior year. Bezos now serves as executive chair of Amazon. The decrease in Jassy’s pay can be attributed to the company’s financial performance, which was described as one of the worst years in its history, with layoffs and a slowdown in sales.

Despite the significant difference in compensation, it’s important to note that Jassy’s salary is still substantial compared to the average CEO pay in the industry. The decline in his pay reflects the challenging circumstances Amazon faced during the fiscal year. However, it’s worth mentioning that executive compensation is complex, considering various factors such as company performance, market trends, and industry standards.

    Proxy Statement and Compensation Analysis

    A proxy statement is a crucial document that publicly traded companies must file with the U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC). It serves as a comprehensive source of information on executive compensation and other significant details for shareholders. Analyzing executive compensation requires a thorough review of the entire proxy statement, which can be accessed directly from the SEC website or the official website of the company in question.

    Understanding Proxy Statements

    A proxy statement goes beyond presenting executive compensation figures. It encompasses a wide range of information that impacts shareholder decision-making, such as corporate governance matters, voting procedures, and proposals. This document is essential for shareholders seeking transparency and accountability from the company they have invested in.

    “Proxy statements provide shareholders with valuable insights into a company’s compensation practices and corporate governance. They enable informed decision-making and promote shareholder engagement.”

    When conducting a compensation analysis, it is pivotal to delve into the proxy statement’s details. This includes examining the compensation philosophy and policies, executive pay structure, equity grants, and performance metrics. By thoroughly reviewing the proxy statement, stakeholders gain a comprehensive understanding of the company’s executive compensation practices.

    For a comprehensive compensation analysis, the following elements should be considered:

    • Base salary
    • Bonuses and incentives
    • Equity grants
    • Stock options
    • Retirement benefits
    • Perquisites

    Additionally, the proxy statement might provide insights into performance measurements, goals, and targets associated with executive compensation. By examining these factors, shareholders can assess the alignment between executive pay and company performance.

    Importance of Proxy Statements

    Proxy statements are crucial documents that provide shareholders with essential information about the agenda and voting decisions of a company’s annual meeting. These statements play a key role in transparently disclosing executive pay practices and providing shareholders with an opportunity to participate in shareholder initiatives.

    Shareholders rely on proxy statements to understand how executive compensation is structured and whether it aligns with the company’s performance. These statements disclose details about salaries, bonuses, stock awards, and other compensation components, allowing shareholders to assess the overall value and appropriateness of the compensation packages.

    Proxy statements are publicly available and accessible to anyone interested in gathering information about a company’s compensation practices. Shareholders can access these documents on the company’s official website or the U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC) website.

    The detailed information provided in proxy statements empowers shareholders to make informed decisions and exercise their voting rights during the annual meetings. By reviewing these statements, shareholders can actively participate in shaping the direction of the company and holding executives accountable.

    SEC Filing Requirement

    Publicly traded companies like AMAZON.COM INC are required to file income statements for executive base pay and bonus with the SEC through the edgar filing system. Executive compensation and pay reports are typically found in the Def 14a documents. These filings ensure transparency and accountability in disclosing executive pay practices.

    Type of FilingDescription
    DEF 14AProxy Statement – definitive information statement
    DEF 14CInformation statement for reorganizing activities
    8-KCurrent report filing

    These filings provide shareholders and the public with valuable insights into the compensation packages of top executives. By making this information readily available, the SEC contributes to the overall transparency and fairness in corporate governance.
