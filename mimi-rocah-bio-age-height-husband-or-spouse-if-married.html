Mimi Rocah Bio, Age, Height, Husband or Spouse If Married

Publish date: 2024-08-02

How well do you know Miriam Rocah, the former Assistant United States attorney? Mimi Rocah, as she is popularly known as, became a famous television personality in 2018 when she was contracted by two widely-known television news networks, MSNBC and NBC News to work as a legal analyst. Prior to her current job position, Mimi started the journey of her presently thriving career as a litigation associate and clerk. Through her perseverance and determination, she eventually, became a public figure who has been honoured with the Women in the Federal Law Enforcement Leadership Award.

Rocah is publicly recognized in the Southern District of New York where she performed extremely well as the Chief of the General Crimes and Narcotics sector and also Chief of the Organized Crime and Racketeering sector. The award-winning legal analyst is also a criminal Justice fellow at Pace Law School, New York. Below are more fascinating details about the former US attorney.

Mimi Rocah: Biography (Age)

The MSNBC legal analyst was born in the United States on 28th of June 1970. Information about her parents and siblings are currently unknown. Mimi Rocah graduated from Harvard University with a Bachelor of Arts degree and later bagged a Juris Doctorate (JD) from the New York University School of Law. Due to her excellent performance, she was inducted into the Order of Coif honorary society of the university.

Rocah landed her first job at a New York-based Law firm, Cravath, Swain and Moore and there, she served as a litigation associate. Soon after, she gained an administrative job of working as a clerk in the office of the United States District Court Judge, John Gleeson, in the Eastern District of New York. During that time, she was also working as a clerk for Chester J. Straub, who was occupying the position of the Senior United States Circuit Judge for the Second Circuit Court of Appeals.

By February 2001, Mimi Rocah became widely recognised, having landed the position of the Assistant United States Attorney in the Southern District of New York; she held the position for a long period of 16 years. During that time, she tried many criminal cases and carried out several prosecutions.

While Mimi Rocah was still an Assistant US attorney, she was named among one of the Chiefs of the White Plains Division as a result of her enormous positive contribution to the society. There, she continued with her fight against crime, working as the chairman of the multi-county task force on human trafficking. In addition, during her time as a Co-Chief of the White Plains Division, the NBC legal analyst also worked as the major liaison officer by collaborating with other agencies in looking into White Plains’ cases in order to reinstate full course of justice.

Interestingly, the leadership roles that Mimi Rocah has been playing in the society didn’t go unnoticed as she was publicly recognized with Women in Federal Law Enforcement Leadership Award in 2016. Also in the same vein, she joined the prestigious Pace Law School situated in New York as a Criminal Justice fellow. There at the Law School, the television personality seizes the opportunity to educate students on Criminal Justice topics such as sex and human trafficking by organizing conferences and seminars.

In 2008, Mimi took to her Twitter account to announce her new job appointment with MSNBC and NBC as their legal analyst. As at the moment, the former Assistant United States Attorney is still remaining with the news broadcasting networks and working as their legal analyst.

Is Mimi Rocah Married? Who is Her Husband or Spouse?

Mimi Rocah is a happily married woman; she tied the knot with her soul mate, David Brian Anders in January 2007. She has been married for over a decade and their union has produced two adorable children, a boy and girl, whose identities are yet unknown. The family currently resides at Scarsdale, New York.

Rocah’s husband, David Brian Anders is a graduate of Fordham University School of Law. Just like his wife, Anders previously served as Assistant United States Attorney of the Southern District of New York. He holds the prestigious position of director of the fund for Modern Court.
